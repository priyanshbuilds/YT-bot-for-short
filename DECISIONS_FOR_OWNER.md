## 2026-07-01 · DECISION #1 — Niche: high-CPM finance (low views) vs universal-curiosity (high views)?

**The data (our channel, `yt_analytics.py`):** science/curiosity videos pull **1,000–1,500 views**; the
first finance video pulled **~67**. 26 subs, 7,457 total views across 25 videos. The "YOUR X is lying to
you" title pattern works *within* a niche (Pencil 1095, Mirror 995) but did not rescue finance.

**Why (market research, not just our data):** this gap is **expected**, not a failure —
- Finance Shorts get **~5–10× fewer views** than entertainment/curiosity (lower "viralability"), BUT
- Finance RPM is **$0.08–0.35 vs $0.03–0.12** for entertainment (**5–10× higher per view**), and finance
  **converts 2–5× better to subscribers**.
- Net: entertainment/curiosity wins on **total views + audience growth**; finance wins on **$/view +
  subscribers + long-term monetization**. (Sources at bottom.)

**So it's a real trade — your call. Three options:**

| Option | What it means | Views | Revenue | Best if you want… |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Pure finance** (current) | keep 100% money topics | low (~50–500) | high RPM, slow | max $/view, patient |
| **B. Pure curiosity** | pivot to science/body/survival/history | high (1k–5k+) | low RPM | fast subs + reach |
| **C. Hybrid (recommended)** | 2 curiosity + 1 finance/day; make finance **number-led & curiosity-framed** (e.g. "This $12 fee is why you're broke") | medium-high | blended | grow the channel AND keep high-RPM money content |

**My recommendation: C (Hybrid).** A new channel needs *reach + subscribers first* (curiosity gets that),
then finance monetizes the audience you built. And research shows finance CAN pull more views when hooked
like curiosity content: **lead with a surprising number**, problem-first, visual number-breakdowns, SFX —
which is exactly our house style. So: keep making finance, but (1) mix in proven curiosity topics to grow,
and (2) reframe finance hooks around a shocking number, not jargon.

**Until you decide, the loop's default (SIGNAL rule in SKILL.md):** lean the daily mix toward curiosity,
keep finance ≤1/day with a number-led, jargon-free hook. Change one line here to pick A/B/C and I'll set it.
