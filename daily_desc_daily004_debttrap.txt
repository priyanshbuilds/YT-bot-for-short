Your minimum payment is engineered to keep you in debt for decades. Here's the receipt.

On a $1,000 card at 22% APR, your "minimum due" is ~2% of the balance — about $25. Of that, only ~$3 touches principal. The rest is interest. The CFPB's own math says: pay only the minimum and that $1,000 takes over 28 years to clear — and you pay ~$2,434 total.

Switch to a flat $100 every month instead, and the same debt is gone in 11 months.
