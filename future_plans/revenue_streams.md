## The three insights that order this list

1. **Per-person value beats per-view value.** A finance email subscriber ($30–100/yr) is worth about
   100,000× a Shorts view ($0.0003). Every video should quietly convert viewers into *owned audience*
   (newsletter, bio link) — that's the asset YouTube can't take away.

2. **Long-form is where finance money actually lives.** $10–25 RPM + clickable affiliate links +
   $6.40/1k affiliate revenue + watch-hours toward YPP. Shorts are the funnel; long-form is the store.

3. **Our structural edge is COGS ≈ 0.** Competitors pay $500–2,000/mo per channel for production we do
   automatically. Every stream above is gravy on a cost base that's just the Claude subscription.

## What NOT to chase (researched and rejected)

- **Credit-card affiliate programs** ($100–405/approval) — closed to small creators; issuers want
  established finance sites. Revisit when we have a site with traffic.
- **Empower-style high-net-worth leads** ($100k-asset requirements) — wrong audience.
- **Snapchat Spotlight** — 50k-follower wall + no Postiz support + separate scheduler needed.
- **Get-rich-quick adjacent sponsor offers** — brand-safety guardrails exist for a reason; one bad
  integration can nuke ad suitability on the whole channel.

## Compliance notes (bake in from day one)

- **FTC:** affiliate disclosure in-video (verbal or on-screen) AND in the first 3 description lines.
- **YouTube:** links inside Shorts are NOT clickable (since Aug 2023) — the funnel is verbal CTA →
  channel page (up to 14 bio links) or QR code in the Remotion outro comp.
- **TikTok:** AI-VO on original scripts is allowed with the AI-disclosure toggle; fully AI-generated
  video is barred from Creator Rewards. Our hand-coded SVG + AI narration is on the right side.
