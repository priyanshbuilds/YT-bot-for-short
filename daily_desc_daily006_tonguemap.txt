The tongue map you learned in school? It's a myth.

Every one of your ~10,000 taste buds can detect all five tastes — sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami. The famous "tip = sweet, back = bitter" diagram came from a 1942 mistranslation of a 1901 German paper by D.P. Hänig. Harvard's Edwin G. Boring turned it into the tongue map that ended up in every textbook.

Scientists definitively debunked it in 1999 (Chandrashekar et al., Journal of Neuroscience) — taste receptors for all five tastes are distributed across the whole tongue.

So the next time someone shows you "the tongue map," you know what to say.
