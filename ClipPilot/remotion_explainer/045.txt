A bear steps onto the trail and locks eyes with you, and your heart drops.
Whatever you do, don't run, because that triggers its chase instinct and it can hit thirty miles an hour.
Instead, stand tall, wave your arms, and speak in a low, calm voice so it knows you're human, not prey.
Now back away slowly. But the right move depends on the species.
If a brown bear charges, play dead and protect your neck.
If a black bear attacks, you fight back with everything you have, because for them, playing dead just makes you an easy meal.
