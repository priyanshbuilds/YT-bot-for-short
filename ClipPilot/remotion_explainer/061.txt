What if you fell feet-first into a black hole? At first, nothing feels wrong, you're just floating in the dark. But as you drift closer, gravity yanks your feet far harder than your head. Then your body starts to stretch, slowly at first, like warm taffy. Scientists actually call this spaghettification, because you'd be drawn into a strand thinner than a hair. And here's the strangest part. To someone watching from far away, you'd never seem to fall in at all. You'd appear to freeze at the edge, glowing redder and redder, until you simply faded from sight.
