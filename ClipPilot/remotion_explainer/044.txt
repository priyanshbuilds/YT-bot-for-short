The snow above you cracks like thunder and a white wall comes roaring down the mountain.
Running won't save you, because an avalanche moves faster than a car.
So as it hits, swim. Thrash your arms in a hard backstroke to fight toward the surface and ride on top of the churning snow.
Then, just before it stops, thrust one hand straight up and cup the other over your face to carve an air pocket.
Now stay still and breathe slow.
Here's the cruel part: once it settles, the snow hardens like concrete in seconds, sealing in anyone who didn't reach the top.
