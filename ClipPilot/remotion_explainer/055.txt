A doctor would lift a patient's eyelid, slide a thin metal pick up behind the eye, and tap it through the bone with a small hammer. Then he'd wiggle it back and forth to sever connections in the brain. The whole thing took about ten minutes, and he ran it like an assembly line, sometimes dozens in a single day. Patients were left calmer, quieter, easier to manage. But many were also hollowed out, unable to think or care for themselves. He even toured the country doing it from a van he called the lobotomobile.
