In the summer of 1518, a woman stepped into a street in Strasbourg and began to dance, and she would not stop. Within days, dozens had joined her, twitching and spinning with no music at all. Soon hundreds were dancing without rest, some for nearly a week straight. You see, the authorities decided the cure was more dancing, so they built a stage and hired musicians. But people kept collapsing, dropping dead from strokes and exhaustion. To this day, nobody truly knows what made an entire town dance itself to death.
