Why can't you remember being a baby? You've been told your baby brain wasn't recording. That's a lie. Yale, 2025 — scientists scanned babies just one year old. When a baby saw a familiar face, the hippocampus lit up. That's the exact memory region adults use. The memories are in there. You just can't retrieve them. Why? Your baby brain grows so fast, new neurons overwrite the path to old memories. It's like a hard drive rewriting its own index. So babies don't forget. Adults just can't find the file.
