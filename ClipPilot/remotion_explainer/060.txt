Deep in the hills of Kentucky lived a family whose skin was a deep shade of blue. You see, a rare blood condition ran in their bloodline, leaving their blood unable to carry oxygen the normal way. And because the area was so isolated, relatives kept marrying relatives, passing the trait down again and again. Children were born with lips the color of plums and skin like a bruise. Neighbors whispered and avoided them for generations. It wasn't until a doctor arrived with a simple pill that their skin slowly faded back to pink.
