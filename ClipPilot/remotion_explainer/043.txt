The cable snaps and the elevator plunges into a free fall.
You've heard you should jump the instant it hits the bottom.
But here's the problem: you'd never time it, and even if you did, you can't out-jump a fall that fast.
So forget jumping. Instead, lie flat on your back in the center of the floor, spreading the impact across your whole body to protect your spine and skull.
Then cover your head.
The reassuring part? Modern elevators have multiple brakes and safety locks, which is why a true free fall is almost impossible.
