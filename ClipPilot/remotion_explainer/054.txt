For thousands of years, people believed a toothache meant a tiny worm was burrowing inside your tooth. You see, the throbbing felt exactly like something alive squirming under the surface. So healers tried to lure the worm out by blowing hot smoke from burning henbane seeds into the mouth. Tiny white threads would seem to wriggle free, and everyone believed the worm had been caught. But those threads were just fibers from the plant itself. The real cause was decay, and the smoke did nothing but leave the rotting tooth still aching.
