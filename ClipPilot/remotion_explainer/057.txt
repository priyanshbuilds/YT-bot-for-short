On an ordinary afternoon in London, a giant vat of fermenting beer suddenly burst its iron hoops. Then the vat beside it ruptured too, and over a hundred thousand gallons exploded outward in a wave fifteen feet high. The flood smashed through brick walls and poured into the cramped basements where families lived. People drowned in the dark, swept off their feet by a tide of porter. Eight died, including a child at a wake. And in the days after, crowds reportedly waded in to scoop up and drink the beer flooding the streets.
