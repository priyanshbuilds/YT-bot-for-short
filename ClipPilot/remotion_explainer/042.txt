You step forward and the ground swallows your boot, then your shin, then your knee.
Every movie says you'll vanish completely, but the truth is stranger.
Quicksand is denser than your body, so you literally cannot sink past your waist.
The real danger is struggling, because each frantic pull creates a vacuum that locks your legs tighter.
So stop moving. Lean back slowly until you're almost floating, and your legs will gently rise free.
Then roll to solid ground.
The catch? If the tide is coming in, that slow patience could be the thing that drowns you.
