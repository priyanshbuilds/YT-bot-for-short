You're swimming in the ocean when a shadow emerges from the deep, and a shark begins to circle you.
Don't splash or swim away, because that mimics wounded prey and invites an attack.
Instead, stay vertical in the water to look less like a seal, and turn smoothly to keep eye contact with the shark.
This shows you're a predator, not a victim.
If it lunges, aim a hard punch at its sensitive gills or eyes.
But the most important rule? Never punch it on the nose—water resistance will slow your arm, and you'll likely just cut your hand on its teeth.
