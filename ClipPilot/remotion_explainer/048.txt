The ice cracks beneath you and you plunge into water so cold it punches the breath from your lungs. You have about one minute before panic takes over, and ten before your muscles quit. So fight the gasp, then turn back toward the hole you fell through, where you know the ice held your weight. Now kick your legs to go horizontal and claw yourself flat onto the surface. Don't stand. Roll away from the hole to spread your weight. And the danger isn't over, because afterdrop hypothermia can still kill you long after you're back on solid ground.
