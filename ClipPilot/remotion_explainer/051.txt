These young women painted glowing numbers onto watch dials, and to keep a fine point, they licked the tips of their brushes. The paint was radium, sold as a miracle, so they painted their nails and even their teeth to glow on dates. But the radium settled into their bones and began eating them from the inside. Their jaws crumbled, sometimes coming away in their hands. And when they sued, the company argued they had died of other causes. Decades later, their bones still glow inside their graves.
