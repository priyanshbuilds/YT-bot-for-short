What if you rode an ordinary elevator straight up into space? For the first minute, it feels like any normal lift. But pass a few thousand feet and your ears start screaming as the pressure drops. Keep climbing, and the air gets so thin you can't pull a full breath. Then around the 60-mile mark, the sky outside turns black in the middle of the day. And if the doors slid open now, you wouldn't explode like in the movies. Instead, the saliva on your tongue would boil away in seconds, and you'd quietly pass out in about fifteen.
