For centuries, the pharmacies of Europe stocked a strange ingredient: ground-up human corpse. You see, doctors believed a body held a person's life force, so consuming it could cure your own ailments. Kings drank tinctures distilled from human skulls to treat seizures. Powdered mummy was stirred into wine for bruises and pain. And fresh blood from executed criminals was sold at the scaffold, still warm, and drunk on the spot. Even a healer who condemned grave-robbing happily prescribed the dead. For nearly three hundred years, Europe's most respectable medicine was quietly built on cannibalism.
