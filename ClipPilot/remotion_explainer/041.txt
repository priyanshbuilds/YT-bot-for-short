If you're swimming in the ocean and suddenly realize you're being pulled away from the shore,
You are likely caught in a rip current.
Your first instinct will be to swim as hard as you can straight back toward the beach,
But you must fight that urge.
A rip current is a narrow, powerful stream of water that can drag even Olympic swimmers out to sea.
Instead, you need to swim parallel to the shore.
Rip currents are usually only a few dozen feet wide.
Once you escape its pull, you can safely swim back to the beach.
