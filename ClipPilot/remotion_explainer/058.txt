Certain monks believed they could become living statues of devotion, so they began starving themselves for a thousand days. First they ate only nuts and seeds to strip away every ounce of fat. Then they switched to bark and roots, and drank a poisonous tea that made them vomit out their fluids. This same tea seeped into their flesh so insects wouldn't eat the body. Finally, sealed alive inside a stone tomb with only a breathing tube, the monk rang a bell each day until the ringing stopped. Some were later found perfectly preserved.
