What if the paper stamped "THIS IS NOT A BILL" is hiding money you should question?

Every time you visit a doctor, your insurer mails you an EOB — an Explanation of Benefits — that quietly lists three numbers per service: the provider's charge, the insurer's allowed amount, and what you actually owe. Most people throw it out without ever opening it.

But if the hospital bill that shows up later asks you for MORE than the EOB's allowed amount, that gap is yours to question. Snap both papers into any free AI and have it line them up side by side — it'll flag every mismatch in seconds.
