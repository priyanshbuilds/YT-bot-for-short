# Competitor Playbook — techniques studied from top YouTubers (append-only, COMPOUNDING)

The **CreatorStudy** run finds the top-performing Shorts in our niches (`yt_top.py`), **`/watch`es** them
frame-by-frame + transcript, and distills WHY they hold attention into this living library. **The producer
reads the TECHNIQUE CATALOG before building every video.** The more we study, the bigger this gets and the
better our videos get — that's the compounding loop.

Confidence: **SEEN-ONCE** (1 winner) → **PATTERN** (≥3 winners / independent strong winners) → **PROVEN**
(we applied it and our AVP/CTR/views rose). Promote PROVEN/strong-PATTERN into SKILL.md ★ SELF-LEARNED RULES.

## § TECHNIQUE CATALOG  (the producer applies these every build)

### 1. Hook (first 1–3s)
- **[PATTERN] Scenario + "you're doing it WRONG" framing.** Gohar: *"your friend gets his first credit card
  but quickly **ruins** his credit score"* (watch a train-wreck). Mark: *"the bank hands you your first
  credit card, here's how to use it **properly**…"* (implies you're using yours wrong). Both name a concrete
  life-moment ("your first card") + open a curiosity loop in <2s. → **This is our "YOUR X is lying to you"
  pattern — keep it, and add the personal life-moment + a trailing open loop.** Evidence: Gohar 19.5M, Mark 10M.
- **[PATTERN, 3 winners] "Have you ever wondered…" curiosity-loop hook.** Zack D.: *"have you ever wondered
  how this trick is done"* — no wind-up, no title card, plunges you straight IN the mystery with the reveal
  visible on screen (magic being performed in extreme close-up at t=0). AdviceWithErin: opens mid-sentence
  in a fake conversation — *"Hi boss I found a better job so I quit"* — you land IN a story already in motion.
  Terra Mystica: *"if YOU got compressed to the size of an ant you might think you would collapse into a
  black hole"* — second-person "what would happen to YOU" thought-experiment. → **Steal: cold-open. No
  intro, no logo, no "today we'll talk about…". Start mid-motion / mid-mystery.** Evidence: Zack D. 128M,
  AdviceWithErin 12.9M, Terra Mystica 63M.
- **[PATTERN] Second-person "YOU" as the story character.** Mark (*"you"*), Zack (*"if you notice"*), Terra
  (*"if YOU got compressed"*), AdviceWithErin (viewer IS the employee getting the reveal). → **The viewer
  should always be the protagonist, not a spectator.** Evidence: 4 winners.

### 2. First frame / cover (the swipe-stopper)
- **[PATTERN] Frame 0 = bold prop + burned-in hook text, readable sound-off.** Gohar: hand-drawn Uno-style
  paper cards (high-contrast, "fun explainer" signal), caption already up. Mark: wall of gold trophies
  (status) + POV hand extending a gold card + caption burned in — all before 1s, keyword "bank" already
  yellow. → **Frame 0 must carry a bold visual + the hook claim as text (our gold badge already does this —
  make the first rendered frame the "cover").**
- **[PATTERN] Frame 0 = EXTREME CLOSE-UP of the "mystery" itself.** Zack D.: t=0 is a tight close-up of a
  hand pouring salt (the trick being performed) — no presenter, no logo, no title card. The mystery IS the
  cover. Terra Mystica: t=0 is a 3D-rendered girl in a bedroom, "compressed" caption already on-screen
  (the transformation subject IS the cover). → **Zoom in HARDER on frame 0. Show the thing we're going to
  explain, not the presenter or a title card.**

### 3. Pacing & editing — the #1 shared engine
- **[PROMOTE — PATTERN, 4 winners across creators + niches] PHRASE-LOCKED visual swapping: change the visual
  on EVERY narration phrase (~1 cut / ~2s).** Gohar: ~15–18 shots in 34s; Mark: ~9–10 hard cuts in 19s, each
  a full LOCATION change; Zack D.: ~20 shots in 33s alternating AGT stage / hand close-up / audience react
  / prop demo; Terra Mystica: ~15 scale-shifts in 30s (ant → bacterium → atom → Schwarzschild). Nobody
  lingers → no static beat where a swipe can happen. → **BIGGEST takeaway for us: our 6-scenes-over-30s
  (~5s/scene) likely lingers too long. Cut/transform the visual on every phrase (~2s), not every sentence.**
  Confidence now high enough to promote as a SELF-LEARNED RULE. (Some of our videos hold only 39–52% AVP.)
- **[PATTERN, 2 winners] Progressive-scale/zoom transitions (visual metaphor IS the concept).** Terra
  Mystica: continuous scale-shift — the concept ("shrink 10,000x") is expressed by the ACTUAL zoom, so
  every phrase = new visual + new spatial level. Gohar (implicit): every "problem" beat pans/cuts to a
  bigger consequence. → **When our topic has a scale/quantity, morph the visual to match the number
  (bar grows, dial rotates, coin stack rises) instead of static cards.**

### 4. Retention devices
- **[PATTERN] Literal word→visual matching:** every spoken claim is ACTED OUT by a matching prop/location
  (Mark: says "weekly shop" while pushing a basket; "buy a house" in front of a house; "30% of credit" while
  tapping at a till. Gohar: drawn Y/N sheet = applications, "BANK" placard = lender. Zack D.: says "watch
  his thumb" while zoom-in on the thumb; says "doofy finger prop" while showing the prop in isolation on
  white background. Terra Mystica: says "size of a bacterium" while showing the character floating in a
  cellular structure). The visuals continuously *prove* the audio → rewards staying. → **Our SVG scene
  must literally depict the words of its phrase.**
- **[PATTERN, 2 winners] Loop-to-start CTA replacing "like & subscribe".** Zack D. closes with *"watch it
  again and see if you can notice"* — literally instructs the viewer to loop, boosting rewatch metric.
  Gohar closes with *"screenshot this"* (save trigger). AdviceWithErin closes with *"Follow for more"* on a
  smiling POV frame. → **End-frame CTA should be an ALGORITHM ACTION (loop / screenshot / save), not a
  generic subscribe. Best variant: "watch again" for videos with a hidden reveal you might miss.**
- **[SEEN-ONCE] Story/character arc** (Gohar): one "friend" makes escalating mistakes → each beat is a new
  "what happens next" gap. **[SEEN-ONCE] Aspiration ladder** (Mark): each cut promises a bigger reward
  (card→score→house→car). **[SEEN-ONCE] "Screenshot this" recap card** (Gohar): full-screen summary at the
  end = save/share + watch-time trigger.
- **[SEEN-ONCE] Two-character skit-dialogue explainer** (AdviceWithErin, 12.9M): same actor plays BOTH
  characters, distinguished by a single prop (glasses = "boss"). Character A does the wrong thing, character
  B reveals the hidden gotcha with a real stat ("only 28% of companies offer immediate match"). Dialogue
  format = built-in curiosity loop (A asks → B reveals). → **Consider a dialogue-skit variant for finance
  topics: e.g. "employee vs boss on 401k vesting" or "customer vs bank rep on overdraft re-ordering". Same
  narration voice, but rendered as a 2-character exchange with a prop-flip.**
- **[SEEN-ONCE] Social-proof reaction shot** (Zack D.): mid-video cuts to an AGT judge's stunned "WOW" face
  reaction. Implies "even the experts are amazed" → reinforces stakes without extra script. → **We can
  fake this w/ a stock "shocked" reaction from Pexels/Openverse cut in on the reveal beat.**

### 5. Visual style
- Winners mix bold props / real B-roll (lo-fi "homemade" or real-location UGC) — high effort reads as
  watchable. Our edge: hand-coded SVG can hit the SAME "one concrete visual per phrase" density *without*
  filming — lean into it (a distinct prop/diagram per phrase), keep high color contrast, presenter-pop color.
- **[NEW — PATTERN] Borrowed / third-party B-roll as the hero visual.** Zack D. uses licensed/borrowed
  AGT stage footage as the *entire* B-roll layer, then cuts in his own hand close-ups only for the reveal.
  Cost: near-zero production. Effect: instant "this feels like real TV" credibility. → **When our topic
  has famous stock footage (a bank sign, a bill, a debate clip), lead with THAT as scene 1's b-roll,
  reserve our bespoke SVG for the "reveal" beat. Content-matched b-roll (per clippilot-broll skill) is
  already wired — use it more aggressively as scene backgrounds under SVG overlays.**
- **[NEW — SEEN-ONCE] Full 3D-CGI narrative visual world** (Terra Mystica): every frame is a rendered
  3D character in a cinematic environment (bedroom → floating in cells → atomic void → black hole).
  Above our production budget for now, but the *technique* — a single consistent "character avatar" that
  travels through a metaphor world — is achievable in Remotion w/ layered SVG parallax. Add to backlog.

### 6. Titles
- **[PATTERN] Ultra-plain, high-search-intent + implied-incompetence.** "What Affects Credit Score?" /
  "How to Use a Credit Card" — a universal object everyone owns but nobody was taught. Curiosity = fear
  you're doing it wrong. No hype adjectives. → keep our deception hook but make titles searchable (include
  the concrete term).

### 7. On-screen text / captions
- **[PATTERN] Burned-in bold captions, the ONE keyword per line highlighted (yellow).** Gohar + Mark.
  Word/phrase-synced, ≤2 lines, heavy outline, readable with sound off; yellow keywords act as skimmable
  headlines. → **We already use gold `#FFD23F` karaoke — make sure the highlighted word is the KEY
  noun/number of the phrase, and captions change on every phrase.**
- **[PATTERN, alt style] Plain white captions, no highlight, 1–3 words at a time.** Zack D. + Terra
  Mystica. Just clean white sans-serif with a soft shadow, bottom or center, 1–2 words on-screen. Works
  because the *visual* is already carrying the meaning — captions are pure sound-off backup, not the
  attention magnet. → **Alt style for videos where the visual is doing the heavy lifting (cinematic
  b-roll, CGI). Our gold-keyword style still wins for factual/data explainers.**
- **[NEW — SEEN-ONCE] Data-label overlays with the exact quantity.** Terra Mystica: on each scale-shift,
  a small overlay states the exact number: *"1.7 micrometers (weight remains same)"*, *"10⁻²⁶ meters (your
  Schwarzschild radius)"*. Makes the video feel authoritative + save-worthy. → **When we quote a number
  in narration, ALSO burn it on-screen as a mini data label (units + parenthetical), not just in the
  karaoke. E.g. "$5,000 (5% match)", "0.01% APY (vs 5.0% HYSA)".**
- **[SEEN-ONCE] Character-labeled dialogue captions** (AdviceWithErin). Every caption is prefixed
  "Employee:" / "Boss:" so viewers can follow the 2-character skit even sound-off. → **If we do a dialogue
  format, prefix each speaker's caption line with their role.**

### 8. CTA & engagement
- Gohar: implicit **"screenshot here"** save-CTA + low-friction community ask (Discord in-joke), not
  "like & subscribe." Mark: **no in-body CTA** — ends on the value payoff ("…to avoid paying interest!").
  → prefer a save-trigger (recap) or a comment question over a generic subscribe ask.
- **[NEW — PATTERN, 3 winners] The end-CTA is an ALGORITHM ACTION, not a subscribe ask.** Zack D.: "watch
  it again" (loop). Gohar: "screenshot this" (save). AdviceWithErin: "Follow for more" ✨ (follow but with
  a soft POV smile, no hard-sell). Terra Mystica: no explicit CTA — payoff frame ("becoming a black hole")
  IS the reward. → **Pick the CTA that matches the video's shape: a hidden-mechanism reveal → "watch it
  again"; a listicle/recap → "screenshot this"; a series-hook → "follow for more"; a hard payoff → no CTA.**

### 9. Length & structure
- Both: front-loaded hook → benefit-forward middle → **payoff on the LAST frame**. Mark = tight 3-tip
  listicle in a single narrative, 19s. Gohar = problem→consequence→solution, 34s. → keep the payoff button
  on the final frame; consider a 3-beat listicle structure; test shorter (19–20s) vs our ~30s.

### 10. Audio / SFX
- **[PATTERN] Continuous run-on VO, NO pauses/breath gaps** (a gap = a swipe). Energetic, slightly playful
  delivery; light music bed low under it; prop-reveal moments synced. Delivery pace does the work, not
  trending audio. → our narration should be continuous + front-loaded; keep SFX synced to reveals.
